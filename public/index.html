Biography

Shayan Monabbati is a PhD candidate in the Systems & Control Engineering program at Case Western Reserve University. His research interests include research interests include the application of artificial intelligence tools in the medical image analysis space, identifying novel computational biomarkers for bile duct cancer, and digital pathology.

September 2015 Journal of Source Themes, 1(1)

Modeling Interference-free Neuron Spikes with Optogenetic Stimulation

This was a study conducted in my 3rd year of undergraduate studies where I investigated the effect of using light to control the rate of opening and closing of opsin proteins. The motivation here was to generate mathematical models that would control the firing time of a particular set of neurons using nothing but light stimulus.
